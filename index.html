Let Us Help You Get Moving

Brigham Physical Therapy is a private physical therapy clinic serving Wichita, Kansas and surrounding areas. Our PT services are available for patients of any age, from pediatric to geriatric. Guided by a sole focus on each patient's individualized treatment, we take the time to apply advanced physical therapy methods to help increase strength, alleviate pain, restore motion and regain function.

The clinic provides a full range of physical therapy treatments for individuals who have experienced injuries or are in need of preventative care.

Meet the Team

Kelsie Brigham, DPT

Director, Brigham Physical Therapy

Kelsie's journey began on a family farm in Garden Plain, Kansas, where she spent her formative years helping with the day-to-day responsibilities. In 2014, she took a significant step in her education by earning her Bachelor's Degree in Exercise Science from Wichita State University. Her dedication to the field led her to pursue a Doctorate of Physical Therapy from the same institution.

With over a decade of experience in the medical field, Kelsie has cultivated a passion for various aspects of physical therapy. Her professional interests span orthopedics, ergonomics, postural pain, gait analysis, running assessments (including fall risk and vestibular issues), treatment for generalized weakness, neurological conditions, pre and postoperative care, sports injury rehabilitation, and preventive measures to ensure her patients' long-term well-being.

Kelsie is presently engaged in advanced medical training in Fascial Counterstrain through the Jones Institute, guided by the expertise of Brian Tuckey. This ongoing pursuit is centered on expanding her knowledge and proficiency in addressing neuro-fascial dysfunction. Kelsie has completed the introduction curriculum of Fascial Counterstrain, Lymphatic Venous techniques and is currently encompassed in the Musculoskeletal system.

Kelsie places immense value on the strong and meaningful relationships she forms while working with her colleagues and patients. Her commitment to patients lies in providing them with a high level of service throughout their physical recovery journey.

Outside of her professional life, Kelsie relishes the moments spent with her husband, son, and daughter. Her love for the outdoors and yoga sessions provide her with a well-rounded and balanced life.



Morgan Ash, DPT

Brigham Physical Therapy

Morgan hails from White City, KS, and currently resides in Wichita with her fiancé, Josh, and their two cats. She earned her Bachelor's degree in Biology from Missouri Southern State University. During her time at MSSU, Morgan was a track and field athlete specializing in the javelin throw. In 2019, she achieved the remarkable feat of winning the NCAA DII national title for the women's javelin throw, following her recovery from an ACL injury. After her tenure in Joplin, Morgan pursued her Doctorate in Physical Therapy at Wichita State University.

Morgan is certified in LSVT BIG, enabling her to provide treatment for patients with Parkinson's disease. Her areas of expertise include post-surgical rehabilitation, fall prevention, balance training, back and neck pain management, sports performance enhancement, and functional exercise. Morgan cherishes her time at Brigham Physical Therapy due to its small, family-like environment, which allows her to deliver high-quality, one-on-one care to her patients.

In her free time, Morgan takes pleasure in trivia, hiking, participating in various sports, reading, and spending quality time with her friends and family.



Kara Ramsey, DPT

Brigham Physical Therapy

Kara grew up in west Wichita where she still lives today. She studied Kinesiology at Kansas State University where she also competed on the women’s rowing team. Kara went on to physical therapy school at Wichita State University, where she met her husband, Korey.

After graduating in 2018, she worked briefly at an assisted living home before coming to work at Brigham in April 2019. After the birth of her two daughters, Kara enjoys working with pregnant and post-partum women and is now certified in Mercier Therapy, which is a deep pelvic organ mobilization to assist women with pelvic pain and infertility. Kara has a strong orthopedic background and loves the challenge of a hard client.

Kara loves the environment that Brigham Physical Therapy creates that allows her to form a personal connection with her patients as well facilitate individual healing through one-on-one treatments. In her spare time, Kara likes to travel and explore new places, spend time outdoors with her husband and daughters, and bake sweet treats!



Lucas Schippers, DPT

Director, Brigham Physical Therapy

Lucas was raised on the west side of Wichita, Kansas. He completed his undergraduate and Physical Therapy studies at the University of Kansas. Following his graduation in May of 2016, he relocated with his wife to Iowa City, IA, where he worked in outpatient physical therapy for five years. After a brief one-year stopover in St. Paul, MN, Lucas returned to Wichita with his wife and two daughters. In July 2022, he embarked on his journey with Brigham Physical Therapy.

Lucas finds immense fulfillment in educating and treating patients with a wide range of conditions, including postural issues, orthopedic problems, core neuromuscular inefficiencies, vestibular deficits, neurological disorders, and individuals looking to enhance their athletic performance. Lucas is currently taking courses through the Institute of Physical Art to advance his skill set.

At Brigham PT, Lucas values the one-on-one setting, where he collaborates with his patients to develop a tailored game plan that enables them to reach their highest potential and achieve their goals.

Beyond his professional life, Lucas cherishes spending quality time with his wife and daughters, as well as with his extended family and friends. He's an avid supporter of the KU Jayhawks and keeps himself occupied with maintaining his house (or, as he calls it, keeping his Schip afloat), which involves tackling various projects and renovations.


For Brigham Physical Therapy, each new day represents an opportunity for continuous learning and the chance to translate that knowledge into meaningful action. Brigham Physical Therapy eagerly anticipates embracing the potential for growth and positive impact that tomorrow holds for each individual patient.
